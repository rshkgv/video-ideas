The camera is locked-off and static throughout — no zoom, no pan. All the energy comes from light and reaction:

2. FRAMES 30–60%: The glow pulses quicken and grow more colorful and frantic, mirroring rising on-screen tension. The person leans forward, gripping a couch cushion, eyes wide, breath held.
3. FRAMES 60–85%: A sudden warm gold flash washes across the whole room — the winner has been announced. The person launches up off the couch, arms thrown up, the snack bowl tipping as popcorn bursts into the air in one natural toss.
4. FRAMES 85–100%: The person is mid-celebration, laughing, head thrown back. A friend's arm and hand enter frame from the side for a high-five — the friend stays a soft-focus, non-identifiable limb only. A small chyron fades in at the bottom: "AMERICAN IDOL · FINALE NIGHT." It remains until the last frame.

Character:
Preserve the person's face, hair, skin tone, and identity exactly from the source image throughout the entire video. Do not alter their appearance.

Style:
