#jamescharles_3

Образ для красной дорожки

Пользователь стоит неподвижно в свободном базовом наряде, вокруг него — незримая команда стилистов: ткань сама натягивается, драпируется, застёгивается невидимыми руками, будто идёт подготовка к красной дорожке в духе бьюти-коллабораций. Камера медленно и непрерывно приближается. В финале — полностью готовый образ, и вокруг начинают вспыхивать частые "вспышки папарацци". Это история про наряд, а не про макияж.

Промпт для видео Kling · Grok 
Create a short cinematic video based on the uploaded image.
The scene is a dark, neutral styling backdrop. The person from the image stands still in a plain, loose base garment, arms slightly away from their body.

Camera and motion structure:
A single slow, continuous push-in from medium shot to close-up drives the entire video:
1. FRAMES 0–25%: Medium shot. The person stands still in the plain garment. Fabric at the hem begins to shift and tighten on its own, as if invisible hands are adjusting it — no hands ever visible, only the fabric's motion.
2. FRAMES 25–55%: The camera continues its slow push-in. The garment visibly reshapes itself — fabric folds, drapes, and cinches into a structured silhouette; a hidden closure fastens itself up the side in one smooth continuous motion. The person remains still, chin lifting slightly as the look comes together.
3. FRAMES 55–80%: Tighter medium shot. The final structural details settle into place — a draped shoulder, a cinched waist — the look is now complete and polished. The person's expression shifts to quiet confidence.
4. FRAMES 80–100%: Close-up. The person holds a poised, camera-ready gaze. Rapid, bright camera-flash pops begin firing from multiple angles just off-frame, strobing across their face and the fabric in quick bursts. The flashes continue until the last frame.

Character:
Preserve the person's face, hair, skin tone, and identity exactly from the source image throughout the entire video. Do not alter their facial structure or identity.
The person's only motion: standing still, a slight chin lift, and settling into a poised final pose as the flashes begin. All transformation happens in the garment, not the body or face.

Key visual events (in order):
- Fabric self-adjusting and tightening with no hands visible
- The hidden closure fastening in one continuous motion
- The structural draping and cinching completing (the hero moment — the garment "finishing itself")
- The rapid paparazzi flash-pops at the very end

Style:
High-fashion editorial realism — think a red-carpet arrival caught by a wall of photographers. The garment's self-assembly must read as tactile and physically real, not a digital morph. The flash-pops should feel like genuine camera strobes, not a graphic effect.

Restrictions:
- Do not change the person's face, hair, skin tone, or identity at any point
- No hands or stylists visible in frame — the garment adjusts itself as if by invisible touch
- No jump cuts — the push-in and the garment's transformation must be one continuous, smooth motion
- The camera-flash pops appear only in the final 20% and continue, strobing, until the last frame
- No speech bubbles
- No social-media UI overlays
- No text captions or graphic overlays of any kind — the flashes are the only visual "effect"
- No aggressive or violent actions
- The backdrop remains the same dark neutral studio throughout — no scene or location changes
