Пользователь выдаёт короткую уверенную шутку-однострочник, будто отвечая на подколку в духе Кевина Харта, и в этот же момент вокруг начинают часто вспыхивать фотовспышки папарацци. Никакого разворота сюжета — просто один заряженный момент уверенности и юмора, с эффектной световой пульсацией на пике. В финале — короткая, стильная плашка с названием вымышленного фильма.

Create a short cinematic video based on the uploaded image.
The scene is a movie premiere red carpet. The person stands in a confident medium shot in front of a blurred step-and-repeat backdrop, a reporter's microphone entering from the bottom edge of frame.

Camera and motion structure:
The camera is completely static throughout — a locked red-carpet interview shot:
1. FRAMES 0–30%: The person stands relaxed and confident, mid-conversation, listening with a slight amused smirk to an implied off-screen question. The microphone bobs very slightly at the bottom of frame, held by an unseen reporter.
2. FRAMES 30–60%: The person delivers a punchy one-liner straight to camera — eyebrows raised, a sharp comic beat, hands gesturing once for emphasis. Their expression is pure confident comic timing.
3. FRAMES 60–85%: The instant the line lands, camera flashbulbs begin popping rapidly all around the edges of frame — a dense, rhythmic flicker of white light bursts, like a wall of paparazzi firing at once. The person breaks into a wide, satisfied grin, leaning back slightly.
4. FRAMES 85–100%: The flashes settle to an occasional pop. The person gives one confident nod to camera. A minimal title card fades in at the bottom: a stylized fictional movie title in clean bold serif with a small film-reel icon, remaining until the last frame.

Character:
Preserve the person's face, hair, skin tone, and identity exactly from the source image throughout the entire video. Do not alter their appearance.
Motion arc, happening only once: relaxed listening → sharp punchline delivery → satisfied grin under the flash barrage → confident nod. No other movement.

Key visual events (in order):
- The punchline delivery (small gesture, raised eyebrow) — the comic beat
- The sudden dense flashbulb barrage synchronized to the punchline landing — the hero moment
- The settle into a satisfied grin as flashes taper off
- The title card fade-in at the very end

Style:
Glossy entertainment-news red carpet photography — warm evening light, shallow depth of field on the blurred backdrop, crisp high-contrast flash lighting during the burst. Confident, glamorous, quick-witted.

Restrictions:
- Do not change the person's appearance, face, clothing, or identity at any point
- The reporter must remain entirely off-screen — only the microphone tip is ever visible, no reporter body or face
- Do not depict any real, identifiable celebrity as a separate character anywhere in the frame
- The flashbulb barrage must read as camera flashes only — no lightning, no fire, no other lighting effect substituted
- The title card appears only in the final 15% and must remain visible until the last frame
- The film title on the card must be entirely fictional — no real movie titles, franchises, or studio logos
- No speech bubbles or captions quoting the joke
- No social-media UI overlays
- No other people clearly visible in the frame
- No jump cuts — each stage flows smoothly and happens only once
