#kevinhart_2

Провальная пародия на открытом микрофоне

Маленький комедийный клуб, вечер открытого микрофона — не большая сцена ростера, а тесное помещение с парой десятков зрителей за столиками. Кто-то в зале снимает происходящее на телефон (POV съёмка от руки, слегка живая, неидеальная). Пользователь на импровизированной сцене пытается изобразить фирменный смех/голос звёздного комика — и обаятельно проваливается: смех выходит не тем, голос срывается. Зал вместо освистывания тепло похохатывает и хлопает — это не ростер, это дружелюбная поддержка. Пользователь сам не выдерживает и заражается смехом от собственного провала.

Create a short cinematic video based on the uploaded image.
The scene is a small comedy club open-mic night, shot as handheld POV footage from an audience member's phone. The person stands on a tiny stage under a single spotlight, mic in hand.

Camera and motion structure:
The camera is handheld POV throughout — a slightly imperfect, lived-in phone-recording feel, small natural drift and micro-shake, never a deliberate pan or zoom:
1. FRAMES 0–25%: The person takes a breath, psyching themself up, grinning at the small crowd. Handheld frame gently drifts as if the filmer shifts their grip slightly.
2. FRAMES 25–55%: The person launches into an exaggerated attempt at a comedian's signature booming laugh/catchphrase — but it immediately cracks and wobbles, voice breaking halfway through in a comically unconvincing way. Their own face registers surprise at how badly it's going.
3. FRAMES 55–80%: The person can't hold a straight face and starts laughing at their own failed impression, mid-attempt, doubling over slightly. The small crowd (heard, not clearly seen — soft warm chuckling and applause) responds with supportive laughter and light clapping, not jeering.
4. FRAMES 80–100%: The person, now fully laughing, gives an exaggerated defeated bow with the mic, grinning ear to ear. A small, handwritten-style "OPEN MIC NIGHT" chalkboard-style overlay fades in at the bottom corner, staying until the last frame.

Character:
Preserve the person's face, hair, skin tone, and identity exactly from the source image throughout the entire video. Do not alter their appearance.
Motion arc, happening only once: psych-up breath → failed impression attempt → self-aware laughter → exaggerated defeated bow. The person is visibly having fun with their own failure — never embarrassed or upset.

Atmosphere and detail:
- Warm string lights and a brick backdrop typical of a small comedy club
- Audience presence conveyed mostly through warm off-screen laughter and applause, not clearly rendered faces
- Handheld phone-camera imperfection throughout: slight shake, natural framing drift, a touch of low-light grain
- The mic occasionally catches the spotlight as the person moves

Style:
Authentic, lived-in open-mic-night realism — warm, intimate, unpolished in a charming way. The comedy comes from the gap between ambition and result, softened by genuine crowd warmth. Never mean-spirited.

Restrictions:
- Do not change the person's appearance, face, clothing, or identity at any point
- Do not depict any real, identifiable celebrity as a separate character anywhere in the frame — the impression is performed by the person themselves, badly, not by a rendered second character
- No mean-spirited, humiliating, or genuinely distressed expression — this is warm, self-deprecating fun with a supportive crowd
- The audience must stay implied via sound and soft blur, never clearly detailed distinct faces
- The "OPEN MIC NIGHT" overlay appears only in the final 20% and must remain visible until the last frame
- No jump cuts — the handheld take is continuous and each beat happens only once
- No speech bubbles or captions quoting jokes
- No social-media UI overlays
- No violent or aggressive actions
