#lakers_3

Попал на джамботрон

Формат «как будто снято с трансляции» — на экране виден джамботрон-фреймы стадиона Lakers с фирменной рамкой «FAN CAM». Пользователь сидит в толпе, не замечая камеру. Есть короткий момент «до»: он тревожно оглядывается по сторонам, пытаясь понять, куда все смотрят и смеются — лицо хорошо видно. Затем до него доходит: это ЕГО показывают на весь стадион. Реакция мгновенная — он вскакивает, кричит, танцует под рёв трибун. Камера статична, как настоящий джамботрон-фид.

Turn the photo of the person into a Lakers fan sitting in the arena stands during a game, purple-and-gold jersey, a thin broadcast-style "FAN CAM" graphic frame visible around the edges of the shot like a jumbotron feed, surrounding fans slightly out of focus, arena lights glowing above.
Промпт для видео Kling · Grok 
Create a short cinematic video based on the uploaded image.
The scene is framed as a stadium jumbotron "Fan Cam" broadcast feed — a thin branded graphic border around the edges of the frame throughout. The person from the image sits among fans in the stands, medium shot, unaware they are on the big screen.

Camera and motion structure:
The camera is completely locked off throughout, exactly like a fixed jumbotron camera feed. No zoom, no pan.
1. FRAMES 0–30%: The person sits casually, watching the game, chatting with people nearby (implied off-frame). They notice nearby fans pointing and laughing at something, looking slightly past the camera.
2. FRAMES 30–50%: Pre-action beat — the person turns their head, looking around left, then right, confused about what everyone finds funny, glancing briefly toward the camera without recognizing it yet. Their face is clearly visible throughout this beat.
3. FRAMES 50–65%: Realization hits — their eyes go wide, mouth drops open, they point at their own chest as if to say "wait, is that me?" A small onscreen "FAN CAM" graphic pulses once in the corner to confirm it.
4. FRAMES 65–100%: The person erupts — jumping up, arms out, spinning once with a huge grin, breaking into an exaggerated little victory dance for the crowd. Nearby fans (implied, out of focus) cheer around them. The "FAN CAM" graphic border remains visible the entire time, and a small purple-gold Lakers wordmark glows steady in the lower corner from FRAME 65% onward.

Character:
Preserve the person's face, hair, skin tone, and identity exactly from the source image throughout the entire video. Do not alter their appearance.
Character arc: casual watching → confused pre-action glances (face fully visible) → wide-eyed realization → joyful spontaneous dance. Comedic, warm, spontaneous — never embarrassed or upset.

Key visual events (in order):
- The confused look-around pre-action beat (this establishes the face clearly, since the jumbotron framing would otherwise make it easy to miss)
- The self-pointing "wait, is that me?" realization
- The onscreen "FAN CAM" graphic pulse confirming it
- The spontaneous victory dance

Style:
Realistic stadium broadcast jumbotron aesthetic — slightly lower fidelity than a cinema camera, a thin graphic border, warm arena lighting bleeding into the frame. Comedic, joyful, spontaneous, like a viral "caught on the big screen" clip.

Restrictions:
- Do not change the person's appearance, face, clothing, or identity at any point
- The "FAN CAM" border graphic is present from the first frame; the Lakers wordmark only appears after FRAME 65% and must remain visible until the last frame
- Camera must remain completely static — no zoom, no pan, no cuts
- The person's face must be clearly visible during the pre-action look-around beat — this is critical
- No speech bubbles or dialogue captions
- No other social-media UI overlays
- No text captions other than the specified "FAN CAM" and wordmark graphics
- No aggressive or violent actions
- Nearby fans stay out of focus / implied — no additional sharply rendered faces
