#smilemakeover

Зеркало ванной

Визуальный якорь — классический ритуал бьюти-трансформации: пользователь стоит перед зеркалом ванной в тёплом утреннем свете, губы сомкнуты, лёгкое волнение на лице. Камера статична, пользователь медленно раздвигает губы в улыбке — и по мере раскрытия улыбки зубы постепенно, по одному, становятся ровнее и белее, будто результат появляется прямо в момент улыбки, а не смены кадра. Финал — широкая, уверенная улыбка, пользователь доволен результатом, глядя в своё отражение.

Промпт для видео Kling · Grok 
Create a short cinematic video based on the uploaded image.
The scene is a warm, softly lit bathroom in front of a mirror. The person stands facing the mirror, medium close-up, face clearly visible throughout.

Camera and motion structure:
Keep the overall shot completely static. No zoom, no camera movement — the smile itself carries the entire arc:
1. FRAMES 0–25%: The person stands with lips closed, a slightly nervous, anticipatory expression, looking at their own reflection.
2. FRAMES 25–55%: The person begins to smile, lips parting slowly. As the smile widens, the teeth visibly and gradually become straighter and brighter, tooth by tooth, in sync with how much the smile has opened — not a sudden switch, a progressive change tied to the smile's own motion.
3. FRAMES 55–85%: The smile continues opening to a full, wide smile, the transformation completing evenly across the whole smile, teeth now straight and bright but still natural-looking.
4. FRAMES 85–100%: The person holds the completed wide smile, eyes brightening with confident satisfaction as they admire the result in the mirror. Hold on this expression to the last frame.

Character:
Preserve the person's face, hair, skin tone, and identity exactly from the source image throughout the entire video. Do not alter their appearance beyond the teeth. The teeth must remain anatomically natural human teeth throughout — straighter and brighter, never unnaturally glowing, oversized, or cartoonish.

Key visual events (in order):
- The closed-lip, anticipatory "before" moment
- The smile opening while the teeth progressively straighten and brighten (the hero moment)
- The completed, natural-looking full smile
- The confident, satisfied held expression at the end

Style:
Warm, intimate, soft-focus bathroom-mirror realism — natural morning light, a personal and quietly emotional beauty-reveal moment rather than a clinical one.

Restrictions:
- Do not change the person's face, hair, skin tone, or identity at any point beyond the teeth transformation
- The teeth must remain anatomically natural human teeth at every stage — no unnatural glow, no exaggerated size or shape, no cartoonish whiteness
- The person's face must remain clearly visible and the visual focus throughout
- No speaking, no singing, no lip-sync, no mouthing words — the smile itself, without any spoken word, is the only action
- No speech bubbles
- The camera must remain completely static throughout — no pans, no zoom, no handheld motion
- The teeth transformation must track smoothly with the smile opening — no jump cut, no instant switch
- No jump cuts of any kind — the entire sequence plays as one continuous, unhurried take
- No other people in frame
- No text captions or overlay graphics
- No social-media UI overlays
