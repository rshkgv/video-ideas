#hauntingadeline

Дневник пишет себя сам

Визуальный якорь — старый кожаный дневник, раскрытый на чистой странице при свече, в готической спальне в духе «Haunting Adeline» (тяжёлые бархатные шторы, тёмно-бордовая роза рядом на столе). Пользователь сидит перед дневником, наблюдая. Камера статична, крупный план на дневник и лицо пользователя рядом. На пустой странице начинают проступать чернильные строки, будто выводимые невидимой рукой — буква за буквой, без видимого пера. Пользователь следит за этим с растущим, зачарованным беспокойством, не касаясь дневника.

Create a short cinematic video based on the uploaded image.
The scene is a candlelit gothic bedroom desk with an open leather journal. The person sits beside it, medium close-up including both their face and the journal's blank page, heavy velvet curtains blurred behind.

Camera and motion structure:
Keep the overall shot static throughout — no zoom, no camera movement. The reveal mechanic is gradual materialization on the page itself:
1. FRAMES 0–30%: The person looks down at the blank journal page, calm, candlelight flickering softly.
2. FRAMES 30–75%: Ink lines begin appearing on the page letter by letter, as if written by an invisible hand — no pen or quill visible, just ink materializing in flowing handwriting, building steadily across the page.
3. FRAMES 75–100%: The person's expression shifts slowly from calm curiosity to unsettled fascination as they watch the final words complete themselves, eyes fixed on the page, one hand rising slightly toward their own chest in a quiet, wordless reaction. Hold on this expression to the last frame.

Character:
Preserve the person's face, hair, skin tone, and identity exactly from the source image throughout the entire video. Do not alter their appearance. The person's face remains clearly visible beside the journal throughout, never obscured by it.

Key visual events (in order):
- The calm look at the blank page, candle flickering
- The ink materializing letter by letter across the page with no visible hand or pen (the hero moment)
- The shift to unsettled, fascinated expression as the writing completes

Style:
Warm candlelight against dark gothic surroundings — intimate, quiet, and unsettling rather than loud or violent. The handwriting itself must read as elegant period-style script, not modern print.

Restrictions:
- Do not change the person's face, hair, skin tone, or identity at any point
- The person's face must remain clearly visible beside the journal throughout every frame
- No speaking, no singing, no lip-sync, no mouthing words — the shifting expression is the only reaction
- No speech bubbles
- The handwriting must appear to materialize with no visible pen, quill, or hand doing the writing — an unseen-force effect, not a literal ghost shown on screen
- The journal page's handwritten text must remain a stylized, illegible period-style script, not readable modern captions
- No jump cuts — the entire sequence plays as one continuous, unhurried take
- No other people in frame
- No social-media UI overlays
