Сквозь стекло кафе

Визуальный якорь — витринное стекло кафе как фильтр папарацци-шторма: пользователь сидит внутри у окна в тёплом свете, а за мокрым от дождя стеклом в темноте улицы начинают вспыхивать редкие фотовспышки, отражаясь и преломляясь яркими бликами по всей поверхности стекла. Камера медленно наезжает, вспышки за стеклом учащаются, свет бликов скользит по лицу пользователя, и в кульминации он спокойно поворачивается к окну и приподнимает бровь с полуулыбкой — невозмутимая реакция человека, привыкшего быть в центре внимания, а не испуг.

The scene is the inside of a café at night. The person sits at a window table, medium close-up, face clearly visible throughout, a rain-streaked glass window pane visible beside them looking out onto a dark street.

Camera and motion structure:
A single slow, continuous push-in for the entire video — no cuts, no whip:
1. FRAMES 0–25%: The person sits calm and relaxed in warm interior light. An occasional faint flash of white light flickers somewhere in the dark street beyond the window, barely noticeable.
2. FRAMES 25–60%: The camera continues its slow push-in as the flashes beyond the glass grow more frequent, their light refracting and streaking across the rain-flecked window pane in sharp white bursts, occasionally rippling across the edge of the person's face via the glass's reflection.
3. FRAMES 60–85%: The flashes outside intensify into a dense, rapid barrage, their reflected light rippling across the glass surface beside the person, though the street itself stays dark and undefined beyond the pane.
4. FRAMES 85–100%: The person turns their head slightly toward the window, calm and unbothered, one eyebrow lifting with a faint, knowing half-smile — fully aware of the storm outside and entirely composed. Hold on this expression to the last frame.

Character:
Preserve the person's face, hair, skin tone, and identity exactly from the source image throughout the entire video. Do not alter their appearance. The person remains the dominant, clearly visible subject in every frame — the window reflections and flashes never obscure their face.

Key visual events (in order):
- The calm interior "before" moment with only a rare distant flash
- The flashes beyond the glass growing more frequent, refracting across the window pane (the build)

- The composed head-turn and knowing half-smile at the end

Style:
Moody, high-contrast café-at-night realism — warm interior tones against cold white flash-light streaking across glass. Restrained, atmospheric, quietly glamorous rather than chaotic.
