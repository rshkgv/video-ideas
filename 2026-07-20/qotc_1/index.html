#qotc

Цитата проступает

Точное значение аббревиатуры #qotc в отчёте не расшифровано (вероятно, «Quote Of The Century» или похожий формат вирусных цитат-каптionов) — идея трактует хэштег как тренд «цитата дня/века», где на экране крупным шрифтом медленно проступает афористичная строка, будто карточка-цитата из вирусного поста. Визуальный якорь — сама типографика: крупный, стилизованный текст цитаты, постепенно проявляющийся поверх спокойного портрета пользователя. Пользователь сидит неподвижно, задумчиво смотрит вдаль, пока текст не проступит полностью, и в конце едва заметно, понимающе улыбается — ни слова.

Промпт для видео Kling · Grok 
Create a short cinematic video based on the uploaded image.
The scene is a calm, moody close-up portrait. The person sits still, medium close-up, gazing thoughtfully slightly off-camera.

Camera and motion structure:
The camera holds a slow, continuous push-in for the entire video — a single deliberate movement:
1. FRAMES 0–30%: The person sits in quiet contemplation, gaze soft and distant, barely moving. The camera begins an extremely slow, almost imperceptible push-in.
2. FRAMES 30–65%: A bold, elegant serif quote-card text begins fading in across the upper-middle portion of the frame, letter by letter growing more opaque — not a speech bubble, a clean stand-alone typographic title-card overlay. The person's expression stays soft and unreadable.
3. FRAMES 65–90%: The quote text reaches full opacity, fully legible, static and centered. The person's gaze shifts slightly toward camera.
4. FRAMES 90–100%: The person's mouth stays closed; a small, knowing, closed-lip smile forms. The camera push-in settles to a stop. Hold on this final quiet beat to the last frame.

Character:
Preserve the person's face, hair, skin tone, and identity exactly from the source image throughout the entire video. Do not alter their appearance. The person remains the visual focus and in frame for the entire video; the text overlay sits above/beside them, never covering their face.

Key visual events (in order):
- The slow, continuous camera push-in
- The quote-card text fading in, letter by letter (the hero moment)
- The text reaching full legibility and holding
- The person's small, knowing smile at the end

Style:
Moody, editorial, aphorism-card aesthetic — muted color grading, elegant serif typography reminiscent of a viral "quote of the day" graphic. Restrained, contemplative pacing.

Restrictions:
- Do not change the person's face, hair, skin tone, or identity at any point
- The person must remain in frame and the clear visual focus for the entire video — the text overlay never covers or obscures their face
- No speaking, no singing, no lip-sync, no mouthing words — a closed-lip smile is the only silent reaction
- No speech bubbles of any kind — the quote must be rendered strictly as a stand-alone title-card/caption text graphic, never inside a speech or dialogue bubble shape
- The quote text must fade in gradually and hold once fully visible — no flashing, no jump cuts in the text's appearance
- The camera push-in must be the only camera movement — slow and continuous, no zoom bursts
- No other people in frame
- No social-media UI overlays beyond the specified quote-card graphic
